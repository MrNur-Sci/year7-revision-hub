1. Ecosystems & Food Webs

Energy moves through food chains and food webs.

🌿 Producer

A plant or algae that makes its own food using photosynthesis.

🐛 Consumer

An animal that eats plants or other animals.

🍄 Decomposer

Breaks down dead organisms and recycles nutrients.

Inputs

Carbon dioxide + water, using light energy.

Outputs

Glucose + oxygen.

Leaf adaptations

Large surface area, chloroplasts, stomata and veins.

4. Fieldwork & Practical Skills

Use quadrats, sampling and graphs to study ecosystems.

Quadrat Sampling

A student counts daisies in five quadrats: 3, 4, 5, 4, 4. Calculate the mean.

Mean = (3 + 4 + 5 + 4 + 4) ÷ 5 = 20 ÷ 5 = 4 daisies per quadrat.

Fair sampling

Why should quadrats be placed randomly?

Random placement reduces bias and makes the sample more representative of the habitat.

5. Challenge

Harder CFU-style thinking.

Final Boss

A farmer removes hedgerows to create larger fields. Explain two possible effects on the ecosystem.

Removing hedgerows can reduce habitats and nesting sites, lowering biodiversity. It can also reduce food sources and disrupt food webs, causing some populations to decrease.
