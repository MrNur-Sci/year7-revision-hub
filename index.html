📖 Key terms — test yourself

Try to define each term before clicking to reveal. Can you get them all?

ForcesSpeed▾
The distance travelled per unit time. Measured in m/s. Speed = distance ÷ time. It is a scalar quantity — no direction needed.
ForcesAcceleration▾
The rate of change of speed. a = Δv ÷ t. Measured in m/s². Positive = speeding up; negative (deceleration) = slowing down. Not the same as speed!
ForcesTerminal velocity▾
The constant speed reached when weight = air resistance (balanced forces, resultant = 0 N). No further acceleration occurs. A skydiver reaches terminal velocity when air resistance grows to match their weight.
ForcesMass vs weight▾
Mass = amount of matter (kg) — same everywhere in the universe. Weight = force of gravity on that mass (N) — changes on different planets. W = m × g. On Earth g = 10 N/kg; on the Moon g = 1.6 N/kg.
ForcesResultant force▾
The single force that represents the combined effect of all forces. Resultant = 0 N → balanced → constant velocity. Resultant ≠ 0 N → unbalanced → acceleration (F = ma).
ElectricityCurrent (I)▾
The flow of electric charge. Measured in amps (A) using an ammeter in series. Current is NOT used up in a circuit — the same current flows at every point in a series circuit.
ElectricityVoltage, current, resistance▾
Voltage (V) — energy transferred per unit charge, measured in volts (V). Resistance (Ω) — opposition to current flow, measured in ohms. Linked by: I = V ÷ R. Higher resistance → lower current for the same voltage.
ElectricitySeries vs parallel▾
Series: one loop, same current everywhere, voltage shared, one break stops all. Parallel: separate branches, each gets full voltage, one break doesn't affect others. Home lights are parallel so they work independently.
EcosystemsProducer / consumer▾
A producer makes its own food by photosynthesis (e.g. grass, algae). A consumer eats other organisms. Primary consumers eat producers; secondary consumers eat primary consumers. Arrows in food chains show the direction of energy transfer — not who eats whom.
EcosystemsPhotosynthesis▾
Plants use light energy to convert CO₂ + H₂O → glucose + O₂. Happens in chloroplasts (contain chlorophyll). Rate is limited by light intensity, CO₂ concentration or temperature — whichever is in shortest supply is the limiting factor.
EcosystemsPollination vs fertilisation▾
Pollination = transfer of pollen from anther to stigma. Fertilisation = fusion of pollen nucleus with ovule nucleus inside the ovary → seed forms. Pollination must happen before fertilisation.
Working Sci.Independent / dependent / control▾
Independent variable = deliberately changed. Dependent variable = measured. Control variables = kept the same to make the test fair. Hypothesis structure: If [IV changes], then [DV] will [change] because [scientific reason].
Working Sci.Hazard vs risk vs precaution▾
Hazard = something that could cause harm (e.g. Bunsen flame). Risk = the chance of harm and how serious it could be. Precaution = action taken to reduce the risk (e.g. wearing goggles, tying hair back).
Working Sci.Anomaly & mean▾
An anomaly is a result that doesn't fit the pattern — likely caused by measurement error. It should be excluded before calculating the mean. Mean = sum of values ÷ number of values. Repeats improve reliability.

✅ Exam tips

Six strategies that make a real difference in the exam room.

📝
Always show working
Write formula → substitute values → calculate. Even if your final answer is wrong, you can earn marks for the correct method and formula.
🔤
Include units every time
Speed in m/s · force in N · mass in kg · weight in N · acceleration in m/s² · current in A · resistance in Ω. Missing units = lost marks.
🔄
Rearrange confidently
v=d÷t → d=v×t and t=d÷v. F=ma → a=F÷m and m=F÷a. W=mg → m=W÷g. I=V÷R → V=IR and R=V÷I. Practise covering each variable in turn.
📊
Know your graphs
D-T graph: horizontal = stopped. S-T graph: horizontal = constant speed (still moving!). This difference catches many students out. Gradient of D-T = speed. Gradient of S-T = acceleration.
⚠️
Avoid the common traps
Mass ≠ weight · current is NOT used up in series · arrows in food chains = energy direction, not "eats" · plants make glucose (don't absorb it from soil) · balanced forces ≠ stopped.
📖
Describe then explain
For extended answers: say WHAT happens first (using data), then WHY it happens (using science). "Speed increased from 2 to 8 m/s because…" scores far more than "speed increased."
