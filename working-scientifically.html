2. Graph Reading and Correlation

Learn A graph helps us see patterns. Correlation means two variables are linked.

Positive correlation

As one variable increases, the other increases.

Negative correlation

As one variable increases, the other decreases.

No correlation

There is no clear pattern.

Try it Read the graph. The points show how far a toy car travelled when released from measured ramp heights.

3. Lab Safety: Hazard, Risk and Precaution

Risk

6. Conclusions and Evaluations

Learn A strong conclusion uses data. A strong evaluation explains how to improve the method.

Conclusion

What pattern did you find? Use numbers as evidence.

Evaluation

How reliable was the method? How could it be improved?

7. Calculate the mean of 4, 5 and 6.

Model answer: (4 + 5 + 6) ÷ 3 = 15 ÷ 3 = 5.

8. Calculate the mean of 10, 12 and 14.

Model answer: (10 + 12 + 14) ÷ 3 = 36 ÷ 3 = 12.

9. Calculate the mean of 20 and 30.

Model answer: (20 + 30) ÷ 2 = 25.

10. Which is the anomaly: 30, 32, 31, 8?

Model answer: 8 is the anomaly because it is far away from 30, 31 and 32.

11. A student repeats a test 3 times. Why is this better?
