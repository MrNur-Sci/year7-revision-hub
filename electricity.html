Big Idea

Electricity flows as a current of charged particles (electrons) around a complete circuit. Voltage drives the current; resistance opposes it. The relationship: Current = Voltage ÷ Resistance (I = V ÷ R).

Section 2

Series and Parallel Circuits

The most important difference: a break in series stops everything. In parallel, each branch is independent.

⚠️ Key misconception: current is used up

Current is NOT used up as it flows around a circuit. In a series circuit, the current is the same everywhere in the loop. Energy is transferred to the components, but the current itself keeps flowing around. Think of it like a water pump — the same water goes round and round.

Series circuit — one loop

🖱️ Click the switch to open/close it!

🔴 Switch open → circuit incomplete → both lamps OFF

✅ Current same everywhere in the loop
✅ If one lamp breaks → ALL lamps go off
✅ Voltage shared between components

Parallel circuit — branches

🖱️ Click the switch on Branch 2 to toggle it!

🟢 Lamp 1 ON · 🔴 Lamp 2 OFF (switch open) — Lamp 1 unaffected!

✅ Each branch gets the full voltage
✅ If one lamp breaks → others stay on
✅ Home lights use parallel circuits

Section 3 · Simulation

Current, Voltage and Resistance

These three quantities are linked by one formula: I = V ÷ R. Changing resistance or voltage changes the current.

I = V ÷ RI = current (A)  |  V = voltage (V)  |  R = resistance (Ω)
current (I) = flow of charge, amps (A) voltage (V) = energy per charge, volts (V) resistance (R) = opposition to flow, ohms (Ω)
⚠️ Current is NOT used up!

The same current flows at every point in a series circuit. What is transferred to the lamp is energy — not current. The current keeps flowing around the full loop.

Section 4

Conductors and Insulators

Conductors let charge flow freely. Insulators don't. This depends on whether the material has free electrons.

⚡ Conductors

Have free electrons that can move and carry charge.

copperironaluminiumsilvergraphite

Used for: wires, circuit tracks, electrodes

🛑 Insulators

Electrons held tightly — cannot carry charge.

plasticrubberwoodglassair

Used for: wire coating, plug casings, handles

Section 5

Static Electricity

Static electricity builds up when charge cannot flow — it's caused by the transfer of electrons between objects.

⚠️ Electrons move — not protons!

When you rub a balloon on hair, electrons (negative charges) transfer from hair to balloon. Balloon becomes negatively charged. Hair becomes positively charged. Protons never move.

positive charge = lost electrons
negative charge = gained electrons
opposite charges → attract
same charges → repel

Section 6 · Simulation

Magnets and Electromagnets

Electromagnets use electricity to create a magnetic field. Unlike permanent magnets, they can be switched on/off and their strength varied.

⚠️ Not all metals are magnetic!

Only iron, steel, nickel and cobalt are magnetic. Copper, aluminium and gold are NOT magnetic — a magnet will not pick up a copper coin.

magnetic materials = iron, steel, nickel, cobalt poles = N and S, strongest parts of magnet attract = N+S pull together repel = N+N or S+S push apart
