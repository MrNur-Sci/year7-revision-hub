2. Series & Parallel Circuits

The arrangement of components changes how the circuit behaves.

Series

One loop. If one component breaks, the whole circuit stops working.

Parallel

More than one branch. Components can work independently.

Circuit Switch Simulator

Open and close the switch to see whether the lamp lights.

Lamp ⚫

Open switch = incomplete circuit = lamp off.

Reveal Check

Why are lights in homes usually connected in parallel?

In parallel, each light has its own branch, so one light can be switched off or break without turning all the others off.

3. Current, Voltage & Resistance

These are the key ideas students often confuse.

Current

Flow of charge. Measured in amps (A). Measured with an ammeter in series.

Voltage

Energy transferred per charge. Measured in volts (V). Measured with a voltmeter in parallel.

Resistance

How difficult it is for current to flow. Measured in ohms (Ω).

Resistance Simulator

Increase resistance and watch current change.

Voltage6 V
Resistance3 Ω
Current2.0 A
For the same voltage, increasing resistance reduces current because it is harder for charge to flow.

Static electricity happens when charges build up.

Reveal Check

Why can a charged balloon stick to a wall?

The charged balloon attracts opposite charges in the wall. This electrostatic attraction can make it stick.

5. Magnetism & Electromagnets

Magnetic fields act around magnets and electromagnets.

Magnet

Has a north and south pole. Opposite poles attract; like poles repel.

Electromagnet

A magnet made using electricity, often a coil of wire around an iron core.

An electromagnet is stronger when current is increased, when there are more coils, or when an iron core is used.

6. Challenge

Harder CFU-style thinking.

Final Boss

A student builds an electromagnet but it is too weak. Suggest two changes and explain why they work.

Increase the current or add more turns of wire around the core. Both changes increase the strength of the magnetic field. Using an iron core would also make the electromagnet stronger.
