2. Distance-Time Graphs

The slope tells you the speed.

Steep line

A steeper line means a faster speed because more distance is covered in the same time.

Flat line

A flat line means the object is stopped. Time passes, but distance does not change.

What does a flat line on a distance-time graph show?

5. Gravity

Gravity is a force that pulls objects towards each other.

⚖️ Mass

The amount of matter in an object. Measured in kilograms (kg). Mass stays the same on Earth and the Moon.

🏋️ Weight

The force of gravity on a mass. Measured in newtons (N). Weight changes on different planets.

🌍 Gravity

A pulling force. On Earth, gravity pulls objects towards the centre of the Earth.

Weight on Different Worlds

Choose a mass and compare the weight on Earth and the Moon.

Mass stays the same. Weight changes because gravity is different.

Reveal Check

Explain the difference between mass and weight.

Mass is the amount of matter in an object and is measured in kg. Weight is the force of gravity on that mass and is measured in N.

8. GCSE Bridge Challenge

Harder reasoning for students who are ready to go further.

Challenge 1: Acceleration and forces

A trolley accelerates slowly even though a student pulls it with a large force. Give two possible reasons.

Possible reasons: the trolley has a large mass, friction is acting against motion, the force is not applied steadily, the surface is rough, or the wheels are not moving freely.

Challenge 2: Explain like a scientist

Explain why two objects fall at the same rate on the Moon but may fall at different rates on Earth.

On the Moon, there is almost no atmosphere, so there is almost no air resistance. Gravity accelerates the objects equally. On Earth, air resistance affects some objects more than others, especially objects with large surface area or low weight, so they may fall more slowly.

Final Boss: Investigation Design

Design an investigation to find out how the height of a ramp affects the speed of a trolley. Include variables, method, repeats and graph.

Independent variable: ramp height. Dependent variable: trolley speed. Control variables: same trolley, same ramp, same distance, same release method. Method: set ramp height, release trolley without pushing, time how long it takes to travel a measured distance, calculate speed using speed = distance ÷ time. Repeat each height 3 times and calculate a mean. Plot ramp height on the x-axis and speed on the y-axis.
